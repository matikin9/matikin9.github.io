Nina Kin

As of mid-2019, I've worked in local government for 12 years. I could say plenty about how and why it's dysfunctional but I'd rather focus on how we're going to fix it.

In 2015, one of my bosses at the time was on the Count of Los Angeles' Open Data Portal Steering Committee. An email had been circulating about hackathons and they were intrigued. My boss tasked me with attending one of these events to find out what it was all about. That's when I attended my first civic hackathon, hosted by Hack for LA. I was hooked immediately. People were excited about solving government's problems! I could leverage my years of government experience to help solve those problems! That led to my heavy involvement in the world of civic tech.
